Almost Maine, Almost Love

Devonte Fuller

It's burdening my soul,
cutting a hole through my heart
All I ask is that you give me what I want
I can't take it anymore, my head pounds with every second
Despite my view of seeming nonchalant

With each and every second I feel worse and worse and worse
Why can't I have what you've neglected me?
This torment, this is vain, all my suf'ring, all my pain
None of which you're ever able to see

That's it? That's all I get? I thoought there'd be much more
After all it did take eleven years
You're lying, where's the rest? This is just a silly game
I'm in no mood for games and childish lore

What do you mean that's all there is, are you saying I'm no good
That I didn't give you every piece of me?
I've returned you all of yours; it barely fit within my doors
Give back my love I gave to you so I have some for me.

6/25/2016

Emily Hayes

I fell in love with a girl. She wore dark, heavy makeup and whenever she smoked or drank, she'd leave a lipstick stain on the cigarette or on the rim of her cup. Her lips were cherry red, or wine colored, or the same pink flush as her cheeks and sometimes I imagined she got her lips to be so red because she kissed the sunsets purple and stole the fires of the sun. But then I saw how shaky her hands were when she applied lipstick on a dirty bathroom mirror, eyeliner streaks on her face, a purse full of makeup remover and concealer to hide the imperfections. Chants of "They won't know unless you tell them. Put on a smile and pretend. It will be okay."

I fell in love with a girl. She talks in her sleep about bright, shiny objects that are sharp as a knife and sting like a cut. She breaks her teeth on jagged glass and she munches on so many pills because the doctors are tired of trying to figure her out, so they prescribe her medicine to cure the anxiety and the migraines and the depression. Around the third refill, the pharmacist warned her to not pop them like candy. They swapped some of them with placeboes and thought she wouldn't notice. That's when the paranoia started up and the nightmares and the long talks about her bright shiny objects. She tells me not to worry, she'll be okay.

I fell in love with a girl. We fought tooth and nail against each her relapses. She was the push into further destruction and I was the pull back into reality. She screamed a million curses at me and damned me to every hell she knew while she spit bile at me on the bathroom floor while I held her over the toliet. She clawed at me, like an animal, swiping across my face and cutting open my cheek. I told her to spit into the bowl and watched as she spit up her lipstick and her pretty words and when she's done throwing up, I make her sit in the bath tub while I scrub at her skin. If I close my eyes, I see her flying off with the ghouls and the ghosts to wreck the heavens, doing her bidding as she pleases. She'll throw up twice again before lying awake because the pain and the guilt keep her up. My hands will shake against her body as I try to tame the beast inside of her head. My hands will shake as I whisper over and over, "We will be okay."

Monet Refuses the Operation

Lisel Mueller

Doctor, you say there are no haloes
around the streetlights in Paris
and what I see is an aberration
caused by old age, an affliction.
I tell you it has taken me all my life
to arrive at the vision of gas lamps as angels,
to soften and blur and finally banish
the edges you regret I don’t see,
to learn that the line I called the horizon
does not exist and sky and water,
so long apart, are the same state of being.
Fifty-four years before I could see
Rouen cathedral is built
of parallel shafts of sun,
and now you want to restore
my youthful errors: fixed
notions of top and bottom,
the illusion of three-dimensional space,
wisteria separate
from the bridge it covers.
What can I say to convince you
the Houses of Parliament dissolve
night after night to become
the fluid dream of the Thames?
I will not return to a universe
of objects that don’t know each other,
as if islands were not the lost children
of one great continent. The world
is flux, and light becomes what it touches,
becomes water, lilies on water,
above and below water,
becomes lilac and mauve and yellow
and white and cerulean lamps,
small fists passing sunlight
so quickly to one another
that it would take long, streaming hair
inside my brush to catch it.
To paint the speed of light!
Our weighted shapes, these verticals,
burn to mix with air
and change our bones, skin, clothes
to gases. Doctor,
if only you could see
how heaven pulls earth into its arms
and how infinitely the heart expands
to claim this world, blue vapor without end.
